Module 4

Motivating & Managing Next-Gen Talent

Careers & Succession Performance Rewards

New-age strategy and tools only matter if they reach people. This module rebuilds careers, performance, and rewards for what next-generation talent actually values — purpose, growth, flexibility, and fairness.

Reimagining careers and succession

The linear career ladder is giving way to lattices: lateral moves, project-based growth, and internal mobility. Succession planning shifts from naming a single heir to building a pipeline of adaptable capability. For next-gen employees, a credible growth path is itself a retention lever.

Performance and rewards, rebuilt

  • Performance management & appraisal — moving from once-a-year ratings toward continuous feedback, coaching, and clear development conversations.
  • Compensation, rewards & recognition — pairing competitive, equitable pay with meaningful, timely recognition and non-monetary rewards that signal value.

The connective idea: motivation for this generation is driven less by tenure-based entitlement and more by growth, autonomy, purpose, and a sense of being seen.

Scenario

A high-performing engineer in her second year tells her manager she's considering leaving. She's well paid and well rated, but says she feels 'stuck' — the only visible path up is a people-management role she doesn't want, and her annual review felt like a formality. The manager escalates to HR, assuming the answer is a raise.

The lens: compensation isn't the lever here. A career lattice (a senior individual-contributor track, project leadership), more frequent development conversations, and timely recognition address the actual drivers — growth, autonomy, and being seen.

Refresh your knowledge?

A career lattice offers growth through lateral and project-based moves and multiple tracks (e.g. individual-contributor and management), in contrast to a single linear ladder.

Continuous performance management replaces or supplements the annual appraisal with ongoing feedback, coaching, and development conversations.

Total rewards combine monetary compensation with recognition and non-monetary rewards; for next-gen talent, growth and purpose often weigh as heavily as pay.
